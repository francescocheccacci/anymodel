Sample dataset description

< Go back to the dataset presentation


This dataset is part of a process for pricing a marine retail insurance product.

Every row in the dataset represents a pool of insured parties with similar characteristics.

The dataset is composed of:

  • A set of 17 columns which represents the characteristics of the pool of insured parties.
  • The response variable, which is named observed, and it represents the number of claims made by the pool of insured parties.
  • An exposure variable, which is named weight, and it represents the number of years that the pool of insured parties has been insured. For example a pool made of 2 insured parties, one that has been covered for 1 year and the other that has been covered for 6 months will have a weight equal to 1.5.
  • Finally we have a variable called fit and it represents the estimate made by our model.

If you take a look at the characteristics of the pools of the insured parties you will actually see 18 columns instead of 17. However column number 18 is not really a characteristic of the pool of insured parties, it is just a 'trick' to have a total of the weight, response and fit columns of our database.
